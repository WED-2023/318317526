👨‍🔬 ABOUT ME

My name is Walter Hartwell White. I’m 51 years old, a former high school chemistry teacher from Albuquerque, New Mexico. I’m married, with two children, and I hold advanced degrees in chemistry. Years ago, I helped found a company called Gray Matter Technologies, but I walked away from it. After being diagnosed with terminal cancer, I chose to take control of my life in the only way I knew how — through science. That path led me into the methamphetamine trade, where I built something far greater than I ever imagined. I’ve always been fascinated by chemistry — by transformation, by precision, by the fundamental reactions that shape everything around us. One person who deeply influenced me was Gus Fring. His discipline, composure, and ability to operate with absolute control left a lasting impression on me — not just as a rival, but as a model of what power truly looks like when it’s quiet. I’ve made mistakes. I’ve lost things I’ll never get back. But I don’t regret the man I became.

Use Case: Searching “Side Effects of Medications 💊”

🧾 Web 1.0: You browse a static website that lists common side effects of a drug, written years ago. The information is outdated and there's no way to ask questions or interact with the content.

🌐 Web 2.0: You Google the term and find blog posts, YouTube videos, and community forums. You read personal experiences and opinions, but the credibility of sources is unclear, and the content is often driven by ads or platform algorithms.

🔗 Web 3.0: You access a decentralized health platform where verified professionals publish transparent, timestamped data. AI tailors the results to your profile using secure data, and results are ranked by community trust — not algorithms or ads.
